About Me


I am a junior at James Madison University studying interactive design through the media arts and design school (SMAD). In addition, I have also completed a minor in general business. I'm an active member in my sorority, Phi Sigma Sigma, where I participate in raising money for our philanthropy, volunteer at community service opportunities, and network with current and previous members at sisterhood events.

During the summer of 2022, I obtained two marketing internships at Bear Chase Brewing Company and Frederick County Parks and Recreation. Through these internships, I was able to expand my network and gain experience creating content for social media platforms, collaborating with different departments throughout the companies, and designing multipage guides for new season activities.

The SMAD courses I'm currently enrolled in have allowed me to conduct user research, usability testing, card sorting, and user interface design. I have also learned skills to code in HTML and CSS programs. My business courses have prepared me with introductions to management, finance, and marketing classes that I will be able to apply with any job I obtain after graduation. I'm so excited to share with you a little bit about who I am and showcase projects I've completed through my SMAD courses and internship opportunities.

User Research

Scout

The scout app was a project completed in my SMAD 201 class in which I conducted user research to find the best way to create my app. After gathering research, I created prototypes of what the app would look like including the landing page, navigation page, and one of the adoptable pets pages.

Scout Prototype






ConServe

The ConServe app was a semester long project I completed in my SMAD 317 class in which we conducted user research and low and high fidelity prototypes. We collected our own research, made an affinity diagram, storyboards, consumer journey maps, and ideation. After we gathered our information, we were able to create a low fidelity prototype and have users test it. After recieving the feedback, we were able to apply the changes and add all of the colors and icons for a high fidelity prototype.
